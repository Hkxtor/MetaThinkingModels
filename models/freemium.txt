<define>
Freemium is a business model where a basic product or service is offered for free, while advanced features require payment. The goal is to attract a large user base with free offerings, then convert a fraction into paying customers.
</define>

<example>
Software & Apps (Low Marginal Cost)
Example: Slack
Free: Limited message history, 10 integrations.
Premium: Unlimited history, priority support ($8/user/month).
Why It Works: Users get hooked on the free version, then upgrade for team needs.
</example>

<example>
Media & Content (Ad-Based or Subscription)
Example: Spotify
Free: Shuffled playlists with ads.
Premium: Ad-free, offline listening ($10/month).
Why It Works: Free users tolerate ads; music lovers pay for convenience.
</example>
