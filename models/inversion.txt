<define>
Inversion is a mental model that involves approaching problems or decisions by considering the opposite of the desired outcome—essentially, thinking backward to identify potential failures or obstacles.
</define>

<example>
Standard Approach: "How can I be more productive?"
Inversion: "What habits make me unproductive?" Avoid distractions; Stop procrastinating by eliminating unclear priorities.
</example>

<example>
Standard Approach: "How can I pick winning stocks?"
Inversion: "How could I lose money in the market?" Stop emotional trading; Spread risk across assets.
</example>

<example>
Standard Approach: "How can I strengthen my relationships?"
Inversion: "What behaviors ruin relationships?" Not listening → Practice active listening; Taking others for granted → Show appreciation.
</example>
