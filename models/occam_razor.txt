<define>
"Among competing hypotheses, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected."
In simpler terms: The simplest explanation is usually the right one.
This principle doesn't guarantee truth but serves as a heuristic to avoid unnecessary complexity.
</define>

<example>
Symptoms: Fever, cough, fatigue.
Complex Hypothesis: The patient has a rare tropical disease + a genetic immune disorder.
Simple Hypothesis: The patient has the flu.
Occam's Razor: The flu is far more likely (fewer assumptions).
</example>

<example>
Issue: Your Wi-Fi isn't working.
Complex Guess: Your ISP is down + the router firmware is corrupted + your device has a virus.
Simple Guess: The router was unplugged.
Occam's Razor: Check the power cord before diving deeper.
</example>

<example>
Observation: Your friend isn't replying to texts.
Complex Assumption: They're angry at you + plotting revenge + ignoring you deliberately.
Simple Assumption: They're busy or didn't see the message.
Occam's Razor: Assume innocence unless proven otherwise.
</example>
