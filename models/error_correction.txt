<define>
Error correction codes are mathematical techniques that detect and fix errors in digital data transmission or storage. They work by adding redundant bits to the original data, creating a self-correcting system.
Key Principles:
Redundancy: Extra bits are added to the data (like a "checksum")
Detection: Identify if errors occurred
Correction: Fix errors automatically when possible
</define>

<example>
Financial Systems
Bank Transactions
Checksum digits in credit card numbers
Example: Your card gets declined if you mistype a digit
</example>
