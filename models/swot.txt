<define>
SWOT Analysis is a strategic planning tool used to evaluate the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats associated with a project, organization, or situation. It provides a structured framework to assess internal factors (Strengths and Weaknesses) and external factors (Opportunities and Threats) to inform decision-making, strategy development, or problem-solving.
</define>

<example>
Business: Starbucks (Retail/Coffee Industry)
Starbucks used SWOT analysis to evaluate its global expansion strategy.  
Strengths: Strong brand recognition, loyal customer base, and extensive global store network.  
Weaknesses: High prices compared to competitors, dependence on the U.S. market for revenue.  
Opportunities: Expanding into emerging markets like India and China, introducing plant-based menu options to attract health-conscious consumers.  
Threats: Intense competition from local coffee chains and fast-food brands, rising costs of coffee beans due to climate change.
Application: Starbucks leveraged its brand strength to enter new markets while addressing weaknesses by diversifying its menu and reducing reliance on premium pricing.
</example>

<example>
Education: A Public School District
A school district applied SWOT analysis to address declining enrollment and academic performance.  
Strengths: Experienced teachers, strong extracurricular programs, and community support.  
Weaknesses: Aging school facilities, limited technology integration in classrooms.  
Opportunities: State funding for STEM programs, growing interest in online learning platforms.  
Threats: Competition from charter and private schools, budget cuts due to local tax revenue declines.
Application: The district invested in technology upgrades and marketed its extracurricular strengths to attract students while lobbying for state funding.
</example>

<example>
Technology: A Startup Developing AI Software
A tech startup used SWOT analysis to position itself in the competitive AI market.  
Strengths: Innovative AI algorithms, agile team, and niche focus on healthcare applications.  
Weaknesses: Limited brand recognition, reliance on a small number of clients.  
Opportunities: Increasing demand for AI in healthcare, potential to expand into other industries like finance.  
Threats: Dominance of large tech companies (e.g., Google, Microsoft), rapid changes in AI regulations.
Application: The startup targeted healthcare partnerships to build credibility and diversified its client base to reduce dependency while monitoring regulatory trends.
</example>
